About Me

Welcome! My name is Brooke Miller and I grew up in Naperville, Illinois. Born to a Chicago suburb but a city girl at heart... I'm a student athlete at Northwestern, and am currently in my junior year. I study at the Medill School of Journalism and couldn't see myself graduating with any other degree. I'm interested in following a career in broadcast journalism, and have developed skills/experience for both in front of and behind the camera. I film and edit videos for fun when I'm not practicing or doing homework. I love to podcast, and even co-host my own named “Whiskers”; About the NU Women’s Soccer Team. I also find joy in photography, and love to document special moments and people using my camera. Most of my favourite work focuses on capturing emotion, and the feelings of the moment. Enjoy!

The Prattic

The student-run comedy club, The Prattic, awaits the start of its weekly show in the attic of 2018 Pratt Ct. on May 26, 2025. Attendees can spot the house by the distinct Christmas lights glowing through the window—and even more so as a line forms closer to the 9:30 show, wrapping from the backyard to the front.

Northwestern Junior and Prattic co-founder Luca Hirsch (21) sits where the comedians will perform, waiting to test the spotlight being used for night’s set. Luca has class on Monday nights until 9 and arrives shortly before the doors open to help set up for the audience.

Friend of the founder’s, Diego Goldfrank (22), takes a drink request from an entering guest. The Prattic entry fee includes a drink of choice, with audience members able to select beer, seltzer, or, on special occasions, beer with a shot of Malört.

Second-time performer Jiho Lee (21) refers to the set he outlined on his phone as he delivers his jokes. When he’s not doing stand-up or operating the boom mic on student-produced films, Jiho is completing his chemical engineering degree.

Founders Luca Hirsch (21), Jackson Heller (21), and Mateo Garcia-Bryce (21) pose in front of their handmade sign indicating the entrance to the attic. The roommates are overjoyed with the night’s performances—including their own—and hope the space offers a welcoming environment for comedians of all levels—something they feel wasn’t available at Northwestern before.
